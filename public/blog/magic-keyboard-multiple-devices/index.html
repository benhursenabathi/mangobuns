Can a Magic Keyboard Connect to Multiple Devices?

July 2026 · 4 min read

Short answer: no. Apple's Magic Keyboard pairs with exactly one device at a time — and the same is true for the Magic Trackpad and Magic Mouse. There are no channel buttons, no device-switching shortcut, no hidden multi-pairing feature.

If you're used to keyboards like Logitech's MX Keys — press a button, jump to another computer — Apple's approach feels like a step backward. Here's why it works this way, and the four real workarounds.

One device at a time, by design

Apple's Magic accessories store a single Bluetooth pairing. Connect your keyboard to a new Mac and it simply forgets the old one. Apple's answer to multi-device workflows isn't multi-pairing hardware — it's ecosystem software (Universal Control, Handoff), which assumes all your devices share one Apple Account.

So if effortless hardware switching is non-negotiable and you don't care about Touch ID or the Apple typing feel, a multi-device third-party keyboard is a legitimate answer. But if you love your Magic accessories, you have four options.

The four workarounds

1. Re-pair manually (free, tedious)

Forget the keyboard on Mac A, connect it on Mac B — through System Settings > Bluetooth each time. It works, but it's a minute of fiddling per switch, multiplied by every device you own. Full steps in our switching guide.

2. The cable trick (free, faster)

Plug the keyboard into the other Mac with its charging cable for a couple of seconds — macOS pairs it automatically, stealing it from the first Mac. Good for occasional moves; see how Magic Keyboard pairing actually works.

3. Universal Control (free, shares rather than switches)

If both Macs use the same Apple Account and sit near each other, Universal Control lets one keyboard and mouse control both — the pairing never moves; input is forwarded. Excellent for side-by-side desks, useless across work/personal accounts or separate rooms. Full breakdown in our Universal Control comparison.
