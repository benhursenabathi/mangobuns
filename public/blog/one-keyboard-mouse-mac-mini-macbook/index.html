One Keyboard and Mouse for Your Mac mini and MacBook

July 2026 · 4 min read

The Mac mini is the best-value Mac Apple sells — which is why so many MacBook owners add one as a desktop. Then comes the surprise: the mini ships with no keyboard, no mouse, and your Magic Keyboard only pairs with one Mac at a time.

Buying a second set of Magic accessories costs real money — a Magic Keyboard plus a Magic Mouse or Trackpad runs roughly $200 or more, and now you have two keyboards fighting for desk space. Here's how to make one set work for both Macs instead.

First: pairing your existing devices with the new Mac mini

The fastest way to get your Magic Keyboard working on a brand-new Mac mini is with a cable:

  1. Plug the Magic Keyboard into the Mac mini with a USB-C (or Lightning) cable.
  2. Wait a couple of seconds — macOS pairs any Magic device that's plugged in, automatically.
  3. Unplug. The keyboard is now Bluetooth-paired to the mini.

Do the same for a Magic Trackpad. For a Magic Mouse, plug the cable into its underside port, wait a moment, and unplug. This is also the trick to remember during initial Mac mini setup if you have no other keyboard around.

The catch: your devices are now paired to the mini — and not your MacBook. Which brings us to the real question.

How to share the devices day-to-day

Option 1: Just use the MacBook as the keyboard (Universal Control)

If the MacBook sits open next to the Mac mini's display, Apple's Universal Control lets the MacBook's built-in keyboard and trackpad control the mini — push your cursor off the screen edge and type away. Free, built-in, and perfect for a two-screen desk, as long as both Macs use the same Apple Account and stay awake. Full details in our Universal Control guide.

Option 2: Re-pair manually every time

Forget the device on one Mac, re-pair on the other — through System Settings or the cable trick above. Free, but tedious enough (multiply by keyboard + trackpad + mouse) that most people either give up and buy duplicates or stop using the second Mac. Our step-by-step guide covers all the manual methods.
