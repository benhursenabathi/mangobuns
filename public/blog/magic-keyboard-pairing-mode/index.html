Magic Keyboard "Pairing Mode": How It Actually Works

July 2026 · 4 min read

If you're hunting for the pairing button on your Magic Keyboard, here's the answer up front: there isn't one. Apple's Magic Keyboard, Magic Trackpad, and Magic Mouse don't have a pairing mode you switch on — which is exactly why pairing them with a new device can feel so confusing.

Here's how discoverability really works on Apple's accessories, and how to fix the classic "my keyboard won't show up in Bluetooth" problem.

The rule: discoverable when on, but not connected

A Magic Keyboard advertises itself to nearby devices whenever two things are true:

  • The power switch (on the back edge — green visible means on) is on, and
  • it is not currently connected to any device.

That second condition trips everyone up. A Magic Keyboard that's paired to your other Mac — even one sitting asleep in a drawer — stays loyal to it and never appears in the new Mac's Bluetooth list. It's not broken; it's just taken.

Making it discoverable, step by step

  1. Break the existing connection. On the device it's currently paired with: System Settings > Bluetooth > hover the keyboard > Forget This Device. (No access to that device? Turning its Bluetooth off also works.)
  2. Power-cycle the keyboard. Slide the switch off, wait a few seconds, slide it on. It now starts advertising.
  3. Connect from the new device. Mac: System Settings > Bluetooth → the keyboard appears under Nearby Devices → Connect. iPad/iPhone: Settings > Bluetooth → tap the keyboard.

The shortcut that always works: plug the keyboard into your Mac with its charging cable (USB-C or Lightning, depending on the model). macOS pairs it automatically in about two seconds — no discovery, no waiting, and it steals the pairing even from another device. Unplug and it keeps working wirelessly. The same trick works for the Magic Trackpad, and for the Magic Mouse via its underside port.

Still not showing up? Quick checklist

  • Charge it. A dead battery looks exactly like a pairing problem. Plug it in for a few minutes.
  • Confirm the switch is actually on. Green visible = on.
  • Check the other Macs in your house. The #1 culprit: it silently reconnected to a previously-paired device the moment you power-cycled it.
  • Toggle Bluetooth on the Mac (off, wait 10 seconds, on) and try again.
  • Use the cable. Seriously — it bypasses every discovery problem at once.
